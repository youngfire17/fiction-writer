# Core Rule

Once a year, the city of Vael Korin votes by anonymous ballot on the seven citizens who most deserve to die, and seven days later it kills them in public — by methods drawn by lot, in the open square, witnessed by anyone who cares to come.

The world is otherwise mundane and recognizably ours: gaslight, broadsheets, telegraphs, hansom cabs, early electric trams, glass-plate photography. There is no magic, no prophecy, no paranormal. Only paper ballots, sworn tellers, iron lots in a bronze bowl, and the long civic habit of believing that this is justice.

Everything else in the world bends around that single fact.
