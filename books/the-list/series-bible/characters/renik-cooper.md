# Renik Cooper

role: secondary antagonist (the night-attempt operator on Day 4; outside the Architect's chain)
pov: no
arc: negative (off-page; he is a man whose lie is already mature when we meet him)
first appearance: ch ~12 (referenced); ch ~13 (on-page in Lamp Alley, briefly)

## Naming convention

- **Renik Cooper** — quay-folk trade-surname, Sailmaker's parish register. He addresses himself in his own head as *Cooper*, the way a man uses his trade name. Brass Ward broadsheets, when his name finally appears in the back of a Council session report, will print it without comment.

## Want / Need

- **Want (external):** Prevent Erys from reaching the Plinth alive. He has, over the last week, become certain that the Architect's clerk-cluster manipulation will be traced back to the Sailmaker's parish committee if Erys reaches dawn on Frostmonth 8 in a condition to speak. He is not protecting Saerin; he is protecting himself and three other committee members from the fallout he assumes is coming.
- **Need (internal):** N/A; his arc is negative and largely off-page. He does not get the page-count to need anything.

## Wound / Lie / Ghost

- **Wound:** Eight years ago, he was the Sailmaker's parish-clerk who wrote a small cluster of ballots against a quay-folk shipwright the parish committee disliked. The shipwright was not placed onto the list — the cluster was too small to land him — but the parish-bell-keeper noticed the signature and called Cooper in. Cooper denied it. The bell-keeper let it go. The fear stayed.
- **Lie:** *If the parish committee survives, I survive; if the parish committee falls, I am the first man down.* The lie is mostly true, which is what makes it a trap. He has been the committee's most cluster-fluent clerk for eight years and he knows the others would name him first if the work came back on them.
- **Ghost:** A single image: the parish-bell-keeper, that morning eight years ago, closing the door of the back-room office and not saying anything for a full minute before letting Cooper go.

## Competence / Contradiction / Flaw

- **Competence:** Clerk-cluster forensics from the inside. He can write the signature; he assumes (correctly) that Veren can read it. He also knows the Lamp Alley grid, the watch's blind-night custom, and the back of the bakery on Cooper's Lane.
- **Contradiction:** None worth a sheet line. He is what he appears to be. The book uses him to clarify that the conspiracy is *not* monolithic — there are independent actors with their own logic — and a contradiction would dilute that work.
- **Flaw:** Panic-prone under specific triggers. The reason the Day 4 attempt fails is not Erys's competence (though she will read it as such) but Cooper's hesitation in the alley when the lamplighter's lantern flickers and he assumes, wrongly, that the watch has broken the blind-night for the first time in living memory.

## Voice

(He gets one or two lines on-page, no more. The drafter does not need a full voice card; this is the parameters for those lines.)

- **vocabulary:** Quay-folk plain. *Aye.* *The girl.* *The job.* He does not name what the job is.
- **sentence length:** Short. Five to seven words.
- **avoids talking about:** The committee. The bell-keeper. The shipwright.

## Physical

Forty-one. Average height, average build, the unremarkable physique that has been his asset for two decades. Sandy hair receding. A neat brown beard. Wears a dark coat the night of the attempt; carries a short fish-gutting knife he has had since fourteen. The knife is the only specific physical detail the drafter needs.

## Arc beats

- **Planted (ch ~12, the night of Day 4):** He waits in Lamp Alley between the eleventh bell and the first bell. Erys is supposed to pass on her way back from a parish meeting Veren sent her to. (The plotter sets the chain.) The lamplighter's lantern flickers at the wrong moment. Cooper hesitates. Erys passes. Cooper turns and walks the other way.
- **Tested (ch ~13, the morning after):** Saerin Korran's response (through intermediaries) reaches Cooper before the second bell of Day 5. The message closes the door on a second attempt. Cooper understands he is on his own. He does not act again in book 1.
- **Resolved (ch ~28-29, after the suspension):** The Council's review (briefly mentioned) names Cooper among the parish-committee members under inquiry. He is alive at the end of book 1. The plotter decides whether he is on-page for the naming or simply present in a broadsheet line.

## Knowledge (at start of book)

- The Sailmaker's parish-committee structure and the clerk-cluster signature he and his colleagues used to place Erys at the top of the list.
- The watch's blind-night custom and Lamp Alley's geometry.
- He does NOT know who paid the committee. He has assumed for two months that the buyer was a Brass Ward party with an Erys-specific grievance. The Architect's name is not in his frame.
