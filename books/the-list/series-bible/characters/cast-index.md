## Biographical note: Aldovan Rell (~30 years deceased; not on-page book 1)

The deeper architect behind the Architect. Born to a Pearl Quarter family with a long shipping pedigree (the family is one of those whose bonded accounts the Architect now draws on; the same family seal appears on the bonded-trust document at Veren's mid-book financial dead-end and on Madam Vell's brooch). Trained as a Pearl Quarter family advocate; never served as a Petitioner himself. Took Saerin Korran on as a private student fifty-five years ago, when Saerin was eighteen and finishing his scholarship year at the Brass Ward law-school. Taught him the Compact's deeper architecture: that the Ceremony's *original* purpose, as drafted in the Year of Knives, was not to name the city's worst but to give a network of senior families, parish-clerk dynasties, and bonded clearing-house officers a yearly opportunity to remove individuals who threatened the harbor economy or the Compact's political stability — without the removal having to be argued, judged, or recorded. Rell believed this design was a moral act, not a cynical one. He believed the harbor economy *was* the city's life, and protecting it through this instrument was a moral necessity.

Rell did not write any of this down. The instruction was oral and ran for twenty-five years. He died of a stroke at his desk thirty years ago; Saerin was forty-three, thirteen years into the Iron-Lily marriage and three years widowed. Rell's papers passed to Saerin (Rell had no surviving children); they are now in the locked drawer of the Iron-Lily House library, the same drawer that holds the Lirien Maris ledger. Saerin has not opened the drawer for the papers in seven years. He has the architecture memorized.

Rell's student-of-a-student in Saern Korin — the inland city whose Ceremony preceded Vael Korin's by a hundred years and whose institutional patterns Vael Korin's were modeled on — is, as of the opening of book 1, the institution's true center. That person is not named in book 1 and does not appear. Book 2 surfaces Rell, the Saern Korin connection, and the wider net.

Status in book 1: iceberg only. No on-page reference; no broadsheet mention; the name appears in the cast-index for the plotter's reference. The Rell name is reserved.

## Foils and ensemble structure (for the plotter)

- **Veren ↔ Saerin.** The student and the teacher. Same competence in different rooms; same love of the Charter; opposite reading of what the Charter is for. The book's central foil pairing. Their on-page scenes together (ch 5, ch ~10, ch ~18, ch ~22, ch ~28) are the book's load-bearing pressure points.
- **Erys ↔ Saerin.** The working-class girl and the man who built the system she is caught in. They meet on Day 4 (ch ~10-11) and Day 5 (ch ~17-18). The Architect's love for her is real; her unguarded trust of him is the flaw he uses. They share no scene after Day 5 until the morning of the Ceremony.
- **Iselen Daran ↔ Eron Veld (off-page).** The new sharper magistrate and the cultivated predecessor. The foil pays off in book 2.
- **Holbren ↔ Saerin.** The two father-figures Erys has. Holbren is silent and present; Saerin is articulate and lethal. Erys has not, until this book, had anyone speak to her at length the way Saerin does on Day 4; she has had Holbren's two-pat-on-the-shoulder kind of love for five years. The book lets the reader feel the difference and then turns it.
- **Veren ↔ Madam Vell.** Two Sailmaker's-parish-born middling-tier operators (Veren by his father; Vell by birth) who chose opposite trades. They share a register (quay-folk substrate under a professional surface) and a competence (parish-clerk-cluster forensics from inside and outside). Their one on-page scene together late in the book (the controlled disclosure of the bonded-account trail) reads as old quay-folk recognizing each other across thirty years of separate work.
