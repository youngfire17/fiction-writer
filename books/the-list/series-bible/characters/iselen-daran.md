# Iselen Daran

role: institutional antagonist / book-2 ally (foil to the retired Architect-protégé magistrate)
pov: no
arc: flat (she is correct at every point in book 1 within the procedural frame; she carries the wound of the ruling she correctly made into book 2)
first appearance: ch 6-7 (Day 3 hearing at the Petitioner's Hall)

## Naming convention

- Public address: **Examining Magistrate Daran**, or **the Magistrate**, in broadsheets and at the Hall.
- Personal: **Iselen** — used by intimates only; she has very few. Veren has heard her addressed as Iselen exactly once, by a senior advocate at a Council banquet six months ago. He noticed it.
- The Cloister parish reads her name with the long *-an* pronunciation; she is from the older middling-tier Brass Ward but was educated at the Cloister school on scholarship, and the formal pronunciation has stuck. Marker of her class-crossing.

## Want / Need

- **Want (external):** Discharge the office of Examining Magistrate as the Charter wrote it — rule on procedural error, refuse on procedural sufficiency, deny private feeling any room in the bench. She has been six months on the bench and has ruled on fourteen filings; she has commuted three and refused eleven. The book opens her on a quiet streak of correctness.
- **Need (internal):** None that surfaces in book 1; her arc is flat. The need that *will* surface (book 2) is to accept that her predecessor's twelve years of rulings were not the work of an independent magistrate but the work of her predecessor's teacher. She does not have this thought in book 1. The book plants its possibility in two scenes (the Day 3 ruling and the Frostmonth 8 morning when she sits at the bench in Counter Square and watches the civic-order suspension move).

## Wound / Lie / Ghost

- **Wound:** Her predecessor, Eron Veld, the magistrate who ruled against Veren's Calden case three years ago, was the Brass Ward solicitor who tutored her for the Council appointments examination eleven years ago when she was a junior clerk. She has not, in six months on the bench, allowed herself to wonder whether his rulings were as clean as his teaching. The wound is dormant.
- **Lie:** *The bench is the bench; what came before me is not mine to relitigate.* This is a procedurally correct disposition that becomes wrong over a sufficiently long time-horizon. The Architect cultivated Veld's bench-customs for twelve years on this principle: a magistrate who refuses to relitigate inherits the cultivation without doing the cultivation herself.
- **Ghost:** Six months ago, the morning of her swearing-in at the Council Hall, Eron Veld attended and shook her hand. He said *the bench will keep you honest; do not let it keep you small.* She has thought about the line at least once a week since. She has not yet noticed that Veld was sworn in by Saerin Korran's recommendation.

## Competence / Contradiction / Flaw

- **Competence:** A magistrate of unusual procedural rigor and unusual pattern-recognition. Reads filings the way Veren reads tallies — for cluster, for signature, for the trace of organized work. The Architect read her early rulings six months ago and recognized her as the kind of person who would notice patterns; the recognition was the trigger for the Erys decision.
- **Contradiction:** A working-class girl who became a Brass Ward magistrate and who still, on cold mornings, walks to the bench in a coat her mother sewed for her at fifteen. Her chambers do not have the polish of her peers; she is not embarrassed about this.
- **Flaw:** She has not yet learned to distrust the people who taught her. The book does not punish her for the flaw in book 1 — she is correct on Day 3, the Charter and the slot rule require the ruling she makes — but the flaw will become the engine of her book-2 arc when she begins to look at the Calden file.

## Voice
- **vocabulary:** Cloister-formal Korresandi at the bench. Brass Ward conversational off the bench, with occasional quay-folk inflection under fatigue (her mother was a Sailmaker's parish laundress). Says *I rule*, *I refuse*, *the filing is sufficient*, *the filing is procedurally lapsed*. Does not say *I am sorry* at the bench. Says it once, to Erys, in the corridor after the Day 3 ruling. The reader notes the deviation.
- **sentence length:** Short and declarative at the bench. Longer and more carefully periodic in private.
- **avoids talking about:** Her predecessor by name. Her own previous filings (she filed three Petitions herself as a Brass Ward solicitor before the appointment; one succeeded). Her mother, who is alive and lives with her.
- **speech tics:** When ruling against a filing, she names the Charter article she is invoking. ("Charter Article Six, the slot rule. I refuse.") This is unusual; her predecessor did not do it. Brass Ward broadsheets have started calling her *the article-citer.*

## Physical

Thirty-eight. Tall (five foot nine), slight, the build of someone who reads more than she walks. Dark hair pulled back severely for the bench, looser in chambers. A small chip in her right front incisor that she covers with a slightly closed-mouth smile until she forgets to. Eyes dark brown. Wears the bench robe (Council-issue, black with a single grey collar-band) at the Hall; off-duty wears unfashionable grey coats and her mother's mended one in cold weather.

## Arc beats

- **Planted (ch 6-7, Day 3 ruling):** She rules against Erys's filing. She is sympathetic; she is bound. The Charter and the slot rule are clear. She invokes Article Six aloud, looks at Erys for a beat longer than she needs to, and refuses. In the corridor after, she says *I am sorry* and means it. Erys hears it and does not register it as the deviation it is.
- **Tested (ch ~26-27, the morning of Frostmonth 8):** She is in the bench room at Counter Square at dawn, on call as the Examining Magistrate. The Chief Magistrate will, that morning, invoke the civic-order suspension at Argent Square. The procedure routes the suspension back through Daran's bench within the hour for the procedural objections she must rule on before the case is reopened. She rules on each one quickly, correctly, with the same article-citing precision. The plotter sets the specific procedural beats.
- **Book-2 seed (a single line, late in book 1):** After the suspension, she will pull the Calden file from the Hall of Tallies' archive. She does not say why. The book ends before she opens it.

## Knowledge (at start of book)

- The Charter in full, the slot rule, the Intent to Represent device, the bench's procedural customs.
- Every filing made in the six months of her tenure, and the bench-rulings on each.
- Her predecessor's reputation as procedurally clean. She does not know what Saerin Korran is to her predecessor's career.
- Veren's reputation by save-rate and by case-record. She has met him twice; she respects him.
- She does NOT know about the Crooked Rule's standing-option market, the Sailmaker's parish-clerk-cluster signature, or the Lirien Maris ledger. She will hit some of this in book 2.
