# Anders Holbren

role: ally (surrogate parent, contrast foil to Saerin Korran on the question of what fatherhood looks like to Erys)
pov: no
arc: flat
first appearance: chapter 1

## Naming convention

- Address: **Master Holbren** by Erys (apprentice-master register, mandatory at the bakery) and by the bakery's regulars. **Anders** by his wife (deceased four years; he still says her name aloud when he locks up at night), his Sailmaker's parish friends, and the parish bell-keeper. The given name does not surface on-page until late in the book; the drafter holds it.
- Surname **Holbren** with the *-en* harbor suffix; quay-folk register; the family has been bakers on Cooper's Lane for three generations.

## Want / Need

- **Want (external):** Keep his bakery and his apprentice safe through Frostmonth. When Erys's name is read at the top of the list, his Want consolidates into one thing: get her through to Frostmonth 9. He has no power in the Brass Ward and no money for the Brass Ward; what he has is the bakery, a Sailmaker's parish vote, and a son's worth of love for a girl who is not his daughter.
- **Need (internal):** None he will admit. He has known, at the level of unexamined feeling, that the Ceremony has been imperfect for as long as he has been a citizen of Vael Korin; he has voted in it every year since his eighteenth and has folded a name in roughly half of those years. The book does not put him in the position of having to examine the feeling. He is a flat character in the technical sense.

## Wound / Lie / Ghost

- **Wound:** His wife, Mira Holbren, died four years ago of the long fever-cycle (the same cycle that took Erys's father, two years earlier in the same cycle). They had no living children. Erys was twelve when his wife died, two years into her apprenticeship. Holbren absorbed the grief by working harder and by treating Erys, increasingly, as a daughter without telling her so.
- **Lie:** *A man does his work and the rest follows.* It has carried him through fifty-six years; the book does not break it.
- **Ghost:** A small image, planted late: Holbren standing in the bakery in the dark, three weeks after Mira's funeral, putting the morning's loaves in the oven by feel because he had not lit the lamps. Erys, then twelve, came down the back stair and lit them without asking. He thanked her and went on working.

## Competence / Contradiction / Flaw

- **Competence:** Thirty-eight years of bread. He knows the Cooper's Lane bakery in the way one knows a house one has lived in alone for forty years. He also knows, by name, every parish-bell-keeper in the Sailmaker's parish; this turns out to be useful when Veren needs a quiet introduction in the back half.
- **Contradiction:** A Ceremony defender at the small-talk level (he has said, at the parish bath-house, that *the list keeps the city honest*) who folds a blank ballot the year his apprentice's father dies, and the year after that, without telling her. He is not aware he is contradicting himself; he is a man who lives by feel.
- **Flaw:** He cannot speak in the Brass Ward. He is fluent in Cooper's Lane and silent on Counter Square. In the climax he will go where he cannot speak and will stand in the front of the crowd anyway.

## Voice
- **vocabulary:** Plain quay-folk, Sailmaker's parish flatness. *Aye* not *yes*. Says *the girl* when he means Erys to other people, *Erys* to her face. Says *the oven* the way a man says *the table.* Does not say *Ceremony*; says *the morning*.
- **sentence length:** Short. Six to nine words. Tells stories in fragments and lets the listener stitch them.
- **avoids talking about:** Mira. The fever year. The folded blanks.
- **speech tics:** Pats Erys on the shoulder twice, briefly, when leaving the room. It is the longest physical contact he allows himself. The drafter deploys this in chapter 1 and again at the climax.

## Physical

Fifty-six. Square-built, heavy through the shoulders, the bakers' lean of a man who lifts flour-sacks all day. Hair the color of wood-ash, thinning. Forearms scarred over with a lifetime of oven-burns; a thumb broken twice (once at fifteen, once at thirty) that he can't fully straighten. Smells of yeast and wood-smoke and the rough soap from the parish bath-house. Wears the same apron the whole book. Limps in the right knee when the weather turns; says nothing about it.

## Arc beats

- **Planted (ch 1):** He hears Erys's name at the Reading and is the only person in the crowd who moves toward her instead of away. He walks her home. He locks the bakery for the day for the first time in seven years and sits with her in the flour-store while she cries.
- **Tested (ch ~16-18):** He goes with Erys (or Veren) to a Sailmaker's parish meeting in the back half to introduce them to the parish-bell-keeper. He is silent at the meeting; he is the credential.
- **Resolved (ch ~27-28):** He stands in the front row of the crowd at Argent Square at dawn on Frostmonth 8. He does not weep. After Erys speaks from the Plinth, he closes his eyes and crosses himself with two fingers — not at the Bowl, because the Bowl has not been brought out — but at her.

## Knowledge (at start of book)

- The Sailmaker's parish-bell-keeper, the parish-bath-house master, three of the parish-committee members at the Sailmaker's Voting House (not by name as committee-members; by name as customers).
- Erys's biographical facts. He took her in at twelve and has had her at his table for five years; he knows her father's death-date, her mother's name (Lirien — he never met her), her chit-numbers, and the line in the parish-charity file that reads *unable to read* (he has known this line for five years and has been quietly teaching her to read at the table after closing).
- He does NOT know about the Crooked Rule, the Architect's role, or the manufactured-case bait. He never will.
