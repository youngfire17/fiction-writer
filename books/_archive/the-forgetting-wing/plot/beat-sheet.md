# Beat Sheet — The Forgetting Wing (book 1)

Target: 80,000 words / 27 chapters / ~2.8-3.2k each / dual-POV alternating (Maren / Sael), with a small number of back-to-back same-POV chapters at high-pressure moments.

POV legend: **M** = Maren; **S** = Sael. Percentages are of total length. Chapter ranges are tentative — see `chapter-list.md` for the chapter-by-chapter map. Every beat ends with `Changes:` — the state shift the chapter(s) must produce.

---

## Act 1 — The body, the assignment, the lock-in (Ch 1-7, ~0-26%)

### B-01 — Opening Image / Hook (0-3%, Ch 1, M)
Maren wakes in Sennet's Alley with a paring knife in her hand and a man at her feet. The Cant grandmother on Helvet Stair is singing the children's rhyme as Maren comes back up the stairs. Maren catalogues what *she* might have done before she catalogues what was done to *her*: the lie in motion on page one.
Changes: the status quo of polite forgetting is ruptured. Maren is now a citizen with information she cannot legally hold.

### B-02 — Theme Stated (~4%, Ch 1 close, M)
The Cant grandmother sings the rhyme's last line — *Wake up clean, wake up gone.* Lo, at the kitchen, asks Maren if she's eaten. Maren lies. The theme — *what do you owe the version of yourself the State has decided to forget* — lands as the rhyme and the small lie in the same breath.
Changes: the reader knows what the book is asking even if Maren cannot yet hear it.

### B-03 — Setup, Sael side / Catalyst on the institutional axis (~5-8%, Ch 2, S)
Sael takes the case at his desk on Tessen Row. The file is routed to Iren without an Audit stamp; the victim's photograph carries a poppy-seed brass pin behind the left ear that no civilian's port should have. Sael lists three things wrong with the file before he reaches the alley.
Changes: the case is procedural anomaly from page one. Sael notes the anomalies and resolves them in favor of the assignment (his flaw, planted live).

### B-04 — Catalyst, Maren side (~9-11%, Ch 3, M)
Wing Field Recovery comes to 17 Helvet Stair to bring Maren in for a citizen-witness interview. Lo reads Sael at the door in three seconds and offers him tea he refuses. Maren walks the four flights down with a borrowed coat. First interview in a Wing intake room. She does not run, does not flinch; she goes still in the way that reads on the Lattice as anomalous.
Changes: Maren is inside the Wing's building for the first time as a person of interest. The investigation has touched her.

### B-05 — Debate / Lattice anomaly (~12-15%, Ch 4, S)
First Sael-POV interrogation chapter. The Lattice reads Maren in patterns Sael has never seen — *soft-three, suppression with no fabrication, two cycles*. He could log the pattern as inconclusive. He logs it as suppression-trending-deceptive. His flaw on the page: he cannot sit with an unresolved interpretation. Treysa Vinn passes him in the corridor and offers a cohort joke he half-laughs at.
Changes: Sael has chosen the case-frame against the evidence; the lie about his job is now operating live.

### B-06 — Break Into Two / Plot Turn 1 — Lock-in (~20-22%, Ch 5-6, M then S)
Maren, released pending further interview, decides she has to find out what she actually did in the three remembered days before the Wing decides for her. She visits Clinic Andren-Vell — Lo's clinic — for a routine port-check that is also a quiet ask. Pell is mentioned by name. In Ch 6 Iren interviews Maren himself (Sael observing) — calm in a way that frightens her differently. Iren tells Sael at end of shift to keep close on the case-craft and that the fourteen-day window is firm.
Changes: Maren commits to investigating her own mind under Wing surveillance. The ticking clock is set: fourteen days to close the file. Door to the ordinary world has closed for both.

### B-07 — B-Story arrives / Theme carrier on the page (~24-26%, Ch 7, M)
Maren goes back to Clinic Andren-Vell after hours through the alley door. She meets Pell on-page for the first time in the back-room dispensary. Pell does not greet her so much as confirm she has been expecting her, unhurried, and presses a stitch film into Maren's hand she did not ask for: *You'll need it. I don't know when.* Pell is the Rememberer foil — the body that carries the theme through the cat-and-mouse middle.
Changes: Maren has an elder she did not have at chapter one. The Rememberer thread is live.

## Act 2A — The cat-and-mouse / the murder solves itself (Ch 8-13, ~27-50%)

### B-08 — Fun and Games / Promise of the Premise (~27-37%, Ch 8-10, M / S / M)
Cross-cut block. The "promise of the premise" of this book is not lightness; it is two prodigies on opposite sides of an interrogation surface. Sael interviews Maren a second time (Ch 9, S); the Lattice fires patterns again and he reads them again as guilt-adjacent. Maren walks Lowmaren in a remembered passage — the grammar drifts; sensory fragments of one of the three days surface (bread-steam smell, a man's voice she does not yet recognize as Iven's, a doorway she will later place as the Cold Archive's freight ingress). Sael in parallel pulls Iven's file (Ch 10, S) and notices the *clerical reassignment, six months prior, Floor Minus Three to Floor Minus Eight* line. Treysa co-reviews the file and names — calmly, professionally — three procedural anomalies. Sael hears her. He does not act.
Changes: by end of block both POVs know the case is not what it was sold as. Both choose to keep working it under the original frame. The reader has the pleasure of two minds at work.

### B-09 — Pinch 1 / First antagonistic blow (~37%, Ch 11, S)
Sael recognizes a procedural fingerprint on the file that should not be there — a senior signing hand is editing the routing above Iren. He brings it to Iren. Iren says *that's a fair question* and does not answer it. The ticking clock tightens: the Wing's fourteen-day window has burned six days.
Changes: Sael now knows the case is being shaped by someone above his supervisor and chooses, again, to stay on the trail rather than escalate. His lie deepens. The institutional antagonist has shown a hand.

### B-10 — Maren reads Sael / the small one as threat (~40-43%, Ch 12, M)
Cant scene. Maren, walking home from a third Wing summons, realizes Sael is following her on foot at distance — not as a tail, as a study. She loses him by going still in a stairwell — using the same trained stillness her aunt taught her, reading him as a tool aimed at her by someone she has not yet identified. She catches him by the wrist coming around a corner. A short, fast-edit, blocking-precise confrontation: Maren *is* the physical threat, briefly, the small one wins, and Sael's Lattice goes wrong in his ear at three meters. He does not log this either. Iren in parallel sees the surname *Telleg* on a routing slip Lo glanced at in ch 7 — a coincidence the orchestrator has chosen to exploit; Lo froze, Maren saw her freeze, neither asked, the deferral chain tightens.
Changes: Maren has read Sael as an instrument, not an enemy. She now believes someone above him aimed him. The anime-faithful physical-threat moment is on the page and will not repeat.

### B-11 — Midpoint Inversion / The case flips (~50%, Ch 13, S)
Sael, alone in the case room at the Wing past curfew, reconciles the body's wound angle, the absence of biometrics in the Wing's archive, the W-I brass pin on Iven's port, the procedural fingerprint above Iren, and the three days Maren remembers that the Wing claims do not constitute a category. He arrives at the inversion: the body was placed for Maren to be found with, and the case was assigned to him specifically. He solves the murder of Iven Telleg — the *who hand* was a Wing-internal action, not a citizen — and that solution opens a question larger than the murder. *Who would have wanted Maren caught remembering, and who would have wanted me to be the one to catch her?* He does not log this. He sits with it for forty minutes by the Sequence clock chimes. He decides to find out before he reports.
Changes: the question of the book flips. The murder is solved by Sael alone; the *who set this up* mystery becomes the engine of the back half. Sael has chosen, for the first time, to act outside protocol. He has not yet admitted that to himself.

## Act 2B — The frame, the personal losses, the system glimpsed (Ch 14-20, ~50-75%)

### B-12 — Pell on the table / Sael in the clinic (~52-55%, Ch 14, S)
Sael, following the stitch supply lead independently, walks into Clinic Andren-Vell. Pell reads him across the front room before he is at the desk. She defeats his Lattice cleanly through a four-minute interview — beta-blockers in her morning tea, Marrow training, the discipline of thirty years on the wrong side of his job. Sael resolves the ambiguity in her favor and leaves; Lo, watching, realizes for the first time the scope of what Pell has been doing.
Changes: Pell now has a Wing file with a face. Sael has had his first encounter with a person his Lattice could not read and chose to read as truth anyway — the small first crack. Lo's withholding becomes harder to maintain.

### B-13 — Maren home / the surname freeze / the deferral cracks (~55-58%, Ch 15, M)
Maren confronts Lo in the kitchen at Helvet Stair. *Did you take stitch?* — *Yes.* The next question — *Who gave it to you?* — Lo cannot ask. Lo asks instead if Maren has eaten. Maren leaves the flat the next morning without explanation. Stitch fragment surfacing in transit: a doorway, a man's hand, a brass pin she did not yet have a name for.
Changes: the household's silence has now cost Maren something the reader can feel. Maren is on her own and will move toward Pell. The book's domestic engine has cracked.

### B-14 — Iven becomes a person / the journal page surfaces (~58-62%, Ch 16-17, M then S)
Maren breaks into Iven Telleg's apartment in Estre-Hand (Ch 16, M). She finds the mice — three generations, two cages, named after Wing protocol numbers. She finds a single sheet of rice paper folded into the wood-shaving liner of one cage: six initials, a date, the word *index* used in place of *day*. The body in the alley becomes a person. She takes the sheet. (In Ch 17, S, Sael takes the tram out to Vell-Outer following an independent lead — the W-I cartridge clearance pin's manufacturer log — and glimpses House Andren through the hedge from the tram. He does not know the relevance. He logs it as *House Andren, attendant in the rear garden, distance ~40 meters, time 14:22*. The reader is told. He is not.)
Changes: Iven is no longer the case; he is a man with thirty-nine names in his head. Sael's mother is on-page for the first time, eight kilometers from his apartment, and he does not know. The Pinch-2 territory begins to load.

### B-15 — Pinch 2 / Carrothenidine tightens, Iren goes quiet, the back-channel acts (~62-65%, Ch 18, S)
The carrothenidine ration to Sael's apartment is delayed by one cycle — a Wing-medical "supply audit." The jaw inflammation breaks through. Sael runs an interview without his usual dose and over-reads the Lattice on a Cant grandmother who does not deserve it. He writes the report and signs it. He brings the Telleg file's third procedural anomaly to Iren. Iren stops asking. The Lattice's back-channel transmits Sael's day-summary to a terminal in an office he has never been to. Treysa files a clean parallel case-summary that does not contradict him and does not endorse him. The initials *H.M.* appear on a routing slip Iren glances at and does not chase. Sael notices the silence around him and resolves it as *ambient case pressure*.
Changes: Sael's institutional supports are quietly withdrawn one by one. He is being shaped. He is also breaking, slightly. The antagonistic force has shown its second hand.

### B-16 — Maren and Pell, the tin opens / Eira's page (~65-72%, Ch 19, M)
Maren reaches Pell in the back room. Pell, finally, opens the tin behind the false back of the dispensary cabinet. She gives Maren the folded sheet — Eira's last date-journal entry — and the small key wrapped in dyed cotton. Eira's page is short. It names the plum tree in Lowmaren that Maren has never walked to. It instructs Maren on what to ask Pell after she has read the page. Pell answers as much as she will answer: Eira was killed; not cardiac; not lost; *killed*. Maren reads the page in Pell's back room. Pell stays in the doorway and does not look away. The room is quiet enough to hear the chiller hum that Marrow Quarter does not have.
Changes: Maren's lie cracks — *my silence killed her* now has to compete with *the Wing killed her on purpose.* She does not yet believe it cleanly. The lie's first real loss.

### B-17 — All Is Lost — Maren / Lo arrives, says some of it, not all (~72-75%, Ch 20, M)
Lo finds Maren at Pell's. She tells her, finally, three of the things Eira asked her to say five years ago. Not all of them. Lo's failure on-page: she hesitates one beat too long before the fourth thing and Maren sees the hesitation. The personal-loss beat for Maren — not the loss of Eira (already lost), but the loss of the version of her childhood in which Lo was uncomplicated. Lo's confession is real and incomplete and Maren will hold it against her for some chapters.
Changes: Maren has the truth about Eira but has lost the household. She has nowhere safe to go home to.

### B-18 — All Is Lost — Sael / Iren reads the file (~73-77%, Ch 21, S)
Iren, at Sael's quiet insistence, reads Iven Telleg's file end-to-end and recognizes — without certainty — the signature pattern in the file routing that he last saw on Hesse's death paperwork fourteen years ago. He does not tell Sael. He goes up to the roof garden in the dark and stands among the tomato plants for an hour. He does not eat for the rest of the day. The next morning he files a Form-Eleven internal inquiry he has not filed in twenty years. (The inquiry will be intercepted before it reaches Audit. The reader is told. Sael is not.) Sael, on the same evening, comes back to his Continuance Street apartment after curfew, takes one dose more carrothenidine than he should, and folds a paper crane. His ghost surfaces — the deputy in the corridor, *we saw you, child* — and for the first time in years he wonders if he was *seen* or *taken*. He turns away from the wondering. Personal-loss beat for Sael: Iren is no longer the unbroken father-substitute. The mentor has gone quiet.
Changes: both protagonists are at their lowest point. The system has won the field. The mentor on each side is partially gone.

## Act 3 — The fight, the room, the irreversible call (Ch 22-27, ~75-100%)

### B-19 — Break Into Three / The plan to meet in the open (~75-80%, Ch 22, M)
Maren, alone in Pell's back room overnight, makes the move the book has been pointing to: she sends Sael a message — through Lo, through a half-spoken Helvet Stair channel, no Wing-monitored line involved — saying she will be at the third plum tree south end in Lowmaren at the hour the Sequence clock chimes nine. She also pulls one item from Eira's page — the post-box key — and decides to open it before she goes. In parallel, the Cant grandmother sings the rhyme again as Maren passes the stairwell. Mirror to chapter one.
Changes: Maren has chosen the meeting. The B-story (theme carried by Pell, by Eira's page, by the Rememberer tendency) becomes the A-plot move. She is no longer reacting.

### B-20 — Finale, set-piece 1 — The fight scene, two chapters before the climax (~80-84%, Ch 23, S)
Sael, en route to the plum-tree meeting, is intercepted in Lowmaren by a Wing Field Recovery pair acting on a Mire-routed order. The order is not a kill order; it is a *retrieve Inquisitor Korrin* order, framed as a medical-supply welfare check now that the carrothenidine has been off-schedule for a week. Sael recognizes the framing for what it is and refuses. Brief, costly, blocking-precise fight in a Lowmaren manufactory yard — the anime-faithful set-piece, but Sael is not the small one; he is two-against-one and the Lattice fires fatigue at sixteen minutes in. He gets clear, hurt, jaw on fire. In parallel, Pell's clinic is raided. The reader sees the raid through the bartender Carrick's window in Lowmaren; Pell is taken without resistance. Lo learns of the raid through a Helvet Stair neighbor.
Changes: Sael has, for the first time in his life, physically refused the Wing on the street. He cannot un-refuse it. Pell has paid the price the bible promised. The clock is at minutes.

### B-21 — Maren at the post-box / what Eira left a year before she died (~84-86%, Ch 24, M)
Maren opens the Lowmaren post-box with the key. Inside is a second sheet of Eira's date-journal — written a year before her last Review, not the morning before — and a small object Eira built to be findable: a photograph (taken by Pell, Pell's stitched cross-thread in the corner) of Eira and a woman Maren does not recognize, standing at the third plum tree, with the Cold Archive freight ingress visible in the background of the frame. The second sheet names what Eira had begun to remember without stitch — the refusal-response — and names the institution she had begun to understand was watching her. *The Wing has rooms below the rooms below.* Maren reads it, refolds it, walks to the plum tree.
Changes: Maren now has *evidence* — Eira's hand, a date, a place, a face — not just Pell's certainty. The deferred grief becomes documented harm. The Cold Archive and Floor Minus Eleven (`defer:book-2`) have been *named in writing* by a citizen the Wing killed for it.

### B-22 — Climax / Two characters in a room making an irreversible call (~88-92%, Ch 25, M then S — back-to-back same-POV halves)
The third plum tree at the south end. Maren is there first. Sael arrives bleeding from the cuff and the jaw. They sit on the stone bench facing the Plate. The chiller compressors hum at the lake-water frequency. The Sequence clock is not audible from here.

This is not a fight scene. It is the decision. Each has half of what the other needs.

Sael has the W-I procedural evidence — the brass pin, the routing-fingerprint, Iren's recognition (not given but inferred), the surveillance frame around Sennet's Alley, the existence of a category of erasure the field staff are not told about. He has, for the first time, said *Wing-Internal* aloud — he does not yet have the term; he calls it *the category I am not cleared to know*. Maren has Eira's pages, Pell's certainty, the photograph, the name of a place under the Wing the citizen literature does not acknowledge. Together they understand: there is a hidden category. There is a Cold Archive. The body was placed. The case was assigned. Someone above Iren — they cannot name him — set both of them in this alley.

They each have one move left and they are not the same move.

Maren says her mother's name out loud for the first time in the book. *Eira Vasse.* The sentence has no hedge and no fragment. She names the killer correctly — *the Wing* — and accepts that this means she cannot fix it in one book and cannot fix it with her own hand. She offers Sael the alliance: she will go underground with Pell's network (what is left of it) and act on the evidence over time, slowly, with cells, with witnesses, with the journal pages still in the Lowmaren post-box system. She asks him to come with her. Sael does not cross.

Sael's grammar cracks for the first time on the page — a fragment in his own voice, not Maren's. *I can't.* He has the truth and he chooses to report it through the chain of command rather than act on it. He believes this is the moral choice. The narrator does not say it is the obedient one; the reader sees. The earnest declaration — anime-faithful, the once-a-book moment — lands on his side: *The institution that raised me cannot be the institution that did this. It cannot.* Maren does not argue. She tells him, plainly, that he is wrong, and that she is not going to convince him because being convinced is something he will have to do for himself in time the Wing has not allotted him. She names *Iven Telleg* — the first time anyone says the name without redaction.

They part on the bench. Maren walks toward the Marrow. Sael walks toward the Wing.
Changes: the immediate murder case is closed by understanding. The deeper case is not closed. Maren chooses to live remembering. Sael chooses obedience and does not know he has been Audit-flagged by it. The alliance the book could have made does not happen; the antagonism is now structural rather than personal.

### B-23 — Resolution / Mirror to the opening image (~94-100%, Ch 26-27, M then S)
**Ch 26 (M, ~3k).** Maren, in a small Marrow Quarter room at dawn, takes a photograph of the Cant grandmother on Helvet Stair singing the rhyme. She has not yet looked at the photographs taped behind the loose pine board behind her lamp; she will, in book 2. Lo finds her in the Marrow at the end of the chapter and Maren lets her in. The final image on Maren's side: the apartment wall above her cot in Cant, in memory, with one photograph now — taken by Maren herself — propped on the rail. She has put one thing forward.

**Ch 27 (S, ~2.5k).** Sael, in his Continuance Street apartment after the report, folds a new paper crane. He does not burn the previous one. The carrothenidine ration has been restored — the Wing has decided he is on the right side after all. Iren has not come to see him. Treysa, in the corridor that evening, asks him how he is. He says *I am fine. To be precise: I have decided what I think.* She does not believe him. She does not say. The final image on Sael's side: the unburned crane on the desk; the closed collar; the count.
Changes: the new equilibrium is two people who know what the Wing is and who have chosen opposite responses to it. The threads the book leaves open are intentional: who runs the Wing-Internal category, what Sael's erased days actually contain, and where Pell is now.

## Genre mandatories — landing log

- **Cold open / violence inside the first 5 pages.** B-01 (Ch 1) — Maren wakes with the knife and the body. Done.
- **Inciting threat by Ch 2-3.** B-03/B-04 — Wing receives the case, comes to the door. Done.
- **Ticking clock.** Fourteen-day Wing case window, set Ch 6, restated Ch 11, Ch 18, Ch 22. Plus carrothenidine ration cycle (Sael) and Wing observation cadence on Maren.
- **Escalation: personal -> local -> systemic.** Maren's body / Lo's silence (personal) -> Pell, the clinic, the journal (local) -> Wing-Internal, the Cold Archive, Floor Minus Eleven (systemic).
- **Mid-book reversal.** B-11 (Ch 13). The case is solved at the wrong layer; the bigger question opens.
- **All-Is-Lost ~75%.** B-17 and B-18 (Ch 20-21). Doubled per POV.
- **Climactic confrontation, morally specific.** B-22 (Ch 25). Genre-bent per intent: a decision, not a fight, with the fight set two chapters earlier (B-20, Ch 23).
- **Brief denouement; cost paid.** B-23 (Ch 26-27). Two short chapters, mirror images of openings.
- **End-of-chapter hooks.** Every chapter ends on a question, reveal, threat, or hard cut — see chapter-list.md.
- **POV cuts accelerate.** Ch 16-17, Ch 19-20-21 are tightly cross-cut; Ch 22-25 trade halves and POVs in faster cadence.
- **Anime-faithful, small-one-wins physical-threat moment.** B-10 (Ch 12). Maren takes Sael at the wrist in a Cant stairwell. The book's only such moment.
- **Sci-fi premise on the page early.** B-01 through B-03 establish the Wing, the port, the Review, the Lattice — by end of Ch 2 the reader knows what kind of book this is.
- **Climax hinges on the speculative element.** The Wing-Internal category and the Cold Archive are the speculative answer to the murder — replace the memory premise with a wrench and the book does not work. Genre contract honored.

## Personal-loss beats — wired and non-duplicated

- **Maren — B-17 (Ch 20).** Lo's incomplete confession. The loss is the version of the household in which Lo was uncomplicated. Bends Maren toward Pell and toward the decision in B-22.
