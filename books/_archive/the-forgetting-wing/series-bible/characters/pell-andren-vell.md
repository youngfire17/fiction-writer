# Pell Andren-Vell

role: ally / Rememberer contact / mentor with a load-bearing lie
pov: no
arc: flat (steadfast in a broken world; pays a price for the steadfastness)
first-appearance: chapter 5 (mentioned through Lo); chapter 8 (on-page, the back room at Clinic Andren-Vell)
aliases: Pell (everyone who knows her); the woman in the dispensary (Wing surveillance log, year four); the seamstress (Marrow cell cover name in years prior)

## Want (external goal)

To get Maren through the next forty days without the Wing identifying Maren as a refusal-response carrier, and to hand off — at exactly the right moment — what Eira asked Pell to give Maren when Maren turned seventeen. Pell has been holding the handoff for five years. She does not yet know if Maren is ready and she is afraid she will misjudge the timing in either direction.

## Need (internal truth)

To accept that withholding from Maren has cost Maren years of grief that Maren has been carrying alone, and that her promise to Eira does not require her to keep the silence — Eira asked her to *give* the thing, not to *guard* it forever. Pell has been treating the promise as the silence rather than the giving.

## Wound

Pell at twenty-six. She was a Wing Cartographer for three years in her early twenties — a Lowmaren scholarship girl who made it through the credential and into a junior fabrication seat on Floor Minus Six. In her third year she fabricated a cartridge for a Vell-Outer citizen whose biometric flag did not match the content category on the work order. She raised it with her supervisor. Her supervisor told her to fabricate it as ordered. She fabricated it. Six weeks later she watched the same citizen's name appear in the Sequence Memorial as a "given day." Pell walked out of the Wing the next morning, surrendered her scholarship debt to the State, and went to live in the Marrow. She has never been back inside the building. She has spent thirty years learning to make stitch.

This is the wound she carries: she built cartridges. She knows what they do. She does not get to call herself an innocent.

## Lie they believe

Pell, unusually, does not believe a load-bearing lie about the world. She is the closest thing in the cast to a flat-arc steadfast character — she sees the Wing clearly, sees herself clearly, sees the choices in front of her. The lie she does carry is more local: *I am the right person to keep this from Maren until I judge the time.* The lie protects her from the smaller and more painful truth: that she has been afraid to give Maren the thing because giving it commits her to a confrontation she may not survive.

## Ghost

The afternoon Eira sat in the back room at Clinic Andren-Vell — three days before her last Review — and handed Pell a folded sheet of paper and a small key wrapped in a square of dyed cotton. Eira said: *If I don't come back, give these to Maren when she has reason to ask.* She did not say what the reason would be. She kissed Pell on the cheek, which she had not done since they were lovers thirteen years before. She left. Pell has the sheet and the key in a tin behind the false back of the dispensary cabinet. She has unfolded the sheet once, when Maren was thirteen and broke down crying in the back room over nothing nameable; Pell refolded the sheet without giving it to her. She has not unfolded it since.

## Competence

- **Stitch synthesis and distribution.** Pell is one of three cutters in Halvern-Estre who can produce a usable paramnestine film from the precursors Lowmaren's manufactories leak. Her films flicker more cleanly than the competition's — fewer false fragments, less liver damage per dose. She has trained two apprentices and outlived both. The plot taxes this when Maren asks for stitch outside the cadence and Pell has to choose between making one and refusing.
- **Reading clinic patients.** Thirty years on the dispensary floor has given her a faster version of the body-reading Lo and Maren do. She reads Sael across the Clinic Andren-Vell waiting room in ch 14 before he has spoken.
- **Marrow Quarter geography and the cell network.** Pell knows the six ingresses, the watchers at each, the active and the compromised cells. She has not been wrong about a watcher in eleven years. She was almost wrong once.

## Contradiction

She is a professional photographer. Not the wall-density compliant kind — she takes formal portraits, Tier Three families, for a small price, in a curtained corner of the dispensary's back room. She has photographed three generations of Helvet Stair. Her portrait of Eira and Maren at six is the only formal photograph in Lo's flat that Maren has not noticed is taken by Pell (the signature mark is small, in the lower right corner: a stitched cross-thread). The contradiction is that the Rememberer who refuses the Wing's framing also runs the most extensive private archive of Tier Three family photographs in Cant. She is documenting the city the Wing is editing. The plot uses this in ch 19 when Pell shows Maren the photograph of Eira she has not let Maren see before.

## Flaw

She has organized her relationship with Maren around the principle that she will be of use when Maren needs her and not before. The flaw is that she has waited too long to give Maren what Eira asked her to give. Two of the things Eira told her to say, Pell could have said three years ago and didn't. The plot makes her pay for this in ch 19-20: by the time she gives Maren the sheet and the key, Maren has had to learn most of what they would have told her the hard way.

## Voice

- **Vocabulary:** Lowmaren-Saxonate with technical pharmacy-Latin and a vein of Marrow argot. She uses *the cut* instead of *the kindness*. She uses Wing-internal terms accurately (cartridge, cartographer, mirror-recall) because she was one. She uses Bureau-era language that nobody else in the book uses — *trial cohort, observation index, refusal pattern* — and the appearance of these words in dialogue is a tell to the reader that Pell knows more than Pell is saying.
- **Sentence length default:** Medium and unhurried. She finishes her sentences and takes a beat before the next one. The pauses are the texture. She has the cadence of a person who has spent thirty years choosing what to say to people who are watched.
- **Avoids talking about:** Her three years in the Wing. The cartridge she built. Eira and her by name in the same sentence — she will say *your mother* and *I* in adjacent sentences but will not put them in the same clause. The tin behind the false back of the cabinet.
- **Speech tics:** *Listen,* — at the start of any sentence she means seriously. *I worked there once* — used twice in the book, deliberately, in different contexts, as a calibrated revelation. *That's an old word* — said when a younger person uses a Marrow term incorrectly; gentle correction is one of her teaching modes.
- **Subtext default:** When stressed, she narrows her focus. She will fix on one thing in the room (a kettle, a thread, a port) and address her listener as if through it. The mask is craft.

## Physical

Fifty-eight. Small, wiry, with the slightly yellow cast around the eyes that long-term stitch users develop — Pell has used stitch once a year for every Review since she left the Wing. She knows the cost; she has chosen it. Her hair is grey-and-rust, kept long and braided in the Lowmaren style. The braid carries a small enamel pin in the colors of the State pharmacy guild — a Wing-era keepsake she has not removed and which she now wears as a quiet provocation to anyone who would recognize it. Her hands are clinic-rough, nails kept very short. The scalp port behind her left ear is the older Bureau-era model, slightly larger than the current one — a tell. She covers it with hair when she leaves the Marrow.

## Arc beats

- **Planted:** ch 5, 8 — Mentioned through Lo; first seen at the clinic when Maren comes for a not-quite-routine port check. Pell is unhurried, unsurprised, and gives Maren a film of stitch she did not ask for. Says, *You'll need it. I don't know when.*
- **Tested:** ch 14 — Sael, working a stitch-distribution lead, walks into Clinic Andren-Vell. Pell is in the front room. She reads him in three seconds, lies cleanly through a four-minute interview while his Lattice fires *confident truth* throughout (she is one of the rare people who can defeat the Lattice — the discipline is Marrow-trained, beta-blockers in the morning tea), and Lo, watching, learns for the first time the full scope of what Pell has been doing. The cost is that Pell has now been seen by Sael and the Wing has a name to attach to a previously anonymous file.
- **Resolved:** ch 19-20 — Pell opens the tin. She gives Maren the folded sheet and the key. The sheet is Eira's last date-journal entry, written the morning before her last Review. The key is to a Lowmaren post-box where Eira left a second sheet — Pell has not opened the post-box. Maren reads the journal in Pell's back room. Pell stays in the doorway and does not look away. The conversation that follows is the conversation Lo has been deferring for five years. Pell says her piece. She does not apologize. (She is a flat arc; she does not change her shape. She does pay: by ch 23 her clinic has been raided and she is in custody. She will appear in book 2 from inside the Wing.)

## Knowledge

- **knows:**
  - Eira was a refusal-response carrier and was monitored.
  - Eira's death was deliberate — Pell has been certain of this since week one. She does not have proof Maren can use; she has the certainty of someone who built cartridges and knows what an *unfortunate procedural variance* looks like when it isn't one.
  - The Bureau predated the Wing and the early trials produced the violence the Charter blames on trauma. (She knows this in outline. She does not have documentation. She would not have used the word *Bureau* aloud until ch 19.)
  - Stitch is produced inside the same manufactories as Lethonal-7. (She has known this for fifteen years. The supply she works with is leaked deliberately. She has theories about who; she does not know.)
  - The Cold Archive exists. (She was Floor Minus Six. She saw the routing.)
- **believes (incorrectly):**
  - That her contact at the Lowmaren manufactory leak is the deepest layer of the supply chain. (It is not. The leak is two layers up, in the Ministry. She does not yet know.)
  - That she has been the right person to hold Eira's package. (She is about to revise this belief.)
  - That she has another year before the Wing identifies her clinic. (She has weeks. Sael's visit accelerates the timeline.)
- **doesn't know:**
  - Wing-Internal as a named category. (She has the substance — she calls them *the unannounced cuts* — but not the word.)
  - That Andren Sault is alive.
  - Floor Minus Eleven as a working theater.
  - The Truth Lattice's back-channel.
  - That the stitch network's tolerance by the Ministry is part of a long internal play that will come into the foreground in book 2.
