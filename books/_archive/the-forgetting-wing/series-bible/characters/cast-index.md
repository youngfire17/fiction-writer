## POV-voice distinction (binding)

The two POV character sheets — `maren.md` and `sael.md` — each open with a **POV voice — distinguishing rules (binding)** section. Those rules are the cast's load-bearing voice contract. The drafter and the voice-scrubber must enforce them on every chapter.

**Maren's distinguishing rules** (see `maren.md`): incomplete sentences and fragments; every assertion shadowed by a second-guess; sensory-first interiority (body-fact precedes label); memory-drift grammar in remembered passages; avoids her mother's name, the word *dead* applied to her mother, and the word *killed* applied to herself.

**Sael's distinguishing rules** (see `sael.md`): enumerated, listed interiority; at least one explicit measurement or count per chapter; complete sentences as default (the grammar mask); Lattice-tap codes in italics, unre-explained; avoids the word *mother*, the word *afraid*, the word *friend*.

A reader should be able to identify whose chapter they are in within the first paragraph based on register alone. The strip-tags self-check from `library/character/voice-and-dialogue.md` has been applied and is documented inside each POV sheet's voice section.

## Faction map (drafter quick reference)

- **Wing field-operations faction** (Iren, Sael, Treysa) — believes the Wing's stated mission. Mostly kept in the dark.
- **Wing internal directorate** (Andren Sault, off-page; staff on Floor Minus Eleven; never on-page) — runs Wing-Internal. The book glimpses the existence, not the personnel.
- **Ministry of Public Cognition senior layer** (Halver Mire, off-page) — running a long game against the directorate using the stitch network as a channel. Not on-page in book 1.
- **The Rememberer tendency** (Pell; small Marrow cells named only in passing) — uses stitch, refuses the Concord framing. Partial knowledge of the deeper structure.
- **The Vasse household / Cant working-class neighbors** (Maren, Lo, Mistress Vell, the Helvet Stair photographers) — Tier Three, no formal knowledge of any of the above, taught not to ask.
