# Framework Notes — The Forgetting Wing (book 1)

## Framework picked

**Three-act spine + Save the Cat beats + scene-and-sequel discipline at the chapter level**, with the midpoint engineered as a question-flip rather than a spectacle, and Save the Cat's "B Story" repurposed as the cross-POV mirror plot (Sael's case-craft against Maren's recovery) since the dual-protagonist structure does not have room for a romance B-story and the intent file forbids one. Hero's Journey was rejected — this is a thriller with two prodigies in a closed institutional system, not a mythic quest; the mentor/threshold/return architecture would flatten the asymmetric stakes between Maren (trying to learn what was done to her) and Sael (trying to defend the system that did it).

Three-act is doing the gross pacing work (Act 1 ~25%, Act 2 ~50% split by the midpoint inversion at ~50%, Act 3 ~25% with climax at ~90% and resolution ~5%). Save the Cat is doing the beat-level work (Hook / Theme Stated / Catalyst / Debate / Break Into Two / Fun and Games / Midpoint / Bad Guys Close In / All Is Lost / Dark Night / Break Into Three / Finale / Final Image). Scene-and-sequel is the chapter-build contract for the drafter: every Maren chapter and most Sael chapters end on a Disaster (No-and-furthermore at pinches, Yes-but at the false midpoint victory, No at most other places) and the following same-POV chapter opens with the Reaction-Dilemma-Decision that hands the next Goal.

## Beats consciously skipped or bent

- **No traditional B-story.** The romance/mentor relationship that usually carries the theme through Fun and Games is replaced by the Maren-Pell line (Rememberer mentor; theme carrier: "what is owed to the version of you the State has decided to forget"). Pell appears at 18% rather than the canonical 22% so the theme has a body before Fun and Games starts.
- **Fun and Games is not light.** Genre would normally let the 20-50% stretch breathe; thriller pacing requires this stretch to be the cat-and-mouse engine. The "promise of the premise" here is two prodigies on opposite sides of an interrogation table — the lightness is replaced with the pleasure of competence on display.
- **Two All-Is-Lost beats.** One per POV (~72-77%). Maren's is the discovery that Lo has been withholding for five years (Lo finally tells her about Eira's last week — incompletely). Sael's is the carrothenidine supply tightening and Iren going quiet on him. Doubling the beat is the cost of dual-POV; the alternative is one POV's All-Is-Lost not landing.
- **Climax is a decision, not a confrontation.** Genre default is a physical/moral climax fused. Per intent.md, the fight scene is two chapters before; the climax is two characters in a room making an irreversible call. This is a conscious genre-bend, justified because the thriller engine has been about who Maren and Sael are, not whether they can outfight each other.
- **The antagonist is partial.** Genre wants the villain faced. The book faces the *system* (the Wing-Internal category, the Cold Archive's existence, the framing) but not the *person* (Andren Sault, Halver Mire). This is the price of book-1-of-series; the visible antagonist on-page in book 1 is the institution, with Mire's hand felt through routing slips and one set of initials.
- **No mid-book ally death.** Pell is raided, not killed (Ch 23). Iren breaks but does not act. The expected pinch-2 mentor death is converted into mentor *silence* on Sael's side and mentor *failure* on Maren's. The body the reader will care about most is Iven's, who dies before chapter 1.

## Arc choices

- **Maren — positive change arc.** Lie: *my silence killed my mother.* Truth: *the Wing killed her on purpose; my silence is grief, not guilt.* Climactic choice (Ch 24): say her mother's name out loud, name the killer correctly, accept she cannot fix it in one book. Cost: she does not get the alliance with Sael; she chooses to live remembering rather than die proving.
- **Sael — negative arc (with positive flickers that fail to convert).** Upper lie: *the Wing is necessary; my job is moral.* Lower lie (deferred to book 2): *my erased days are what the Wing told me they were.* Climactic choice (Ch 24): given the W-I evidence and a partner to act with, he reports it through chain of command instead of acting on it. He believes he chose the moral path; the reader sees he chose the obedient one. Cost: he loses Maren, gains an Audit-flag he does not know about, folds a paper crane and does not burn the previous one.

## Open queries to the user

Drafter should consult the world-builder before committing prose for that page; if no canon emerges, suggest a single image-and-instruction (the plum trees in Lowmaren, third from south end — already canon in Maren's ghost) so the page reads as both confession and inheritance.
