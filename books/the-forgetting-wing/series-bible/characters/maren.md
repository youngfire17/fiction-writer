# Maren Vasse

role: co-protagonist
pov: yes
arc: positive
first-appearance: chapter 1
aliases: Mar (her aunt Lo's name for her, used twice a chapter at most)

## POV voice — distinguishing rules (binding)

These rules differentiate Maren on a single paragraph from Sael. The drafter and the voice-scrubber are bound by them.

1. **Incomplete sentences.** Maren thinks in fragments. A clause begins; she abandons it before the verb. Period or em-dash where the predicate would go. Roughly one fragment per three sentences in interior passages.
2. **Second-guessing.** Every assertion is shadowed by a correction or a hedge in the next clause. *She knew the man. She had not known the man. She had only thought she did, for a second, the way you think you know any face in a doorway.* The retraction is the texture.
3. **Sensory-first interiority.** Maren registers the world through skin, smell, sound, weight before she names what she is feeling. If a scene contains an emotion, the prose finds it through a body fact (the cold of the railing, the bread-smell from the Lowmaren wind, the wet seam of her sleeve) before any label.
4. **Memory drift.** When she is inside a remembered passage, the grammar drifts: present-tense fragments, fewer determiners, less narrative connective tissue. The reader feels the difference without being told.
5. **Avoids:** her mother's name, the word *dead* applied to her mother, the word *killed* applied to herself. Uses circumlocutions: *what happened to her*, *what I did or did not do*.

## Want (external goal)

To find out what she actually did in the three days she should not remember — specifically whether her hand was the one that put the paring knife into the man in Sennet's Alley — and to do it before the Wing catches her remembering at all.

## Need (internal truth)

To stop treating her mother's death as a private moral fault — *if I had asked, she would still be here* — and accept that her mother was killed by an institution, not lost by a daughter who failed to notice. She has been carrying the wrong grief for five years.

## Wound

When Maren was twelve, her mother Eira died during what was officially Eira's fifteenth Review. The clinicians called it cardiac. Maren, in the week before, had noticed her mother becoming strange — agitated, asking Maren odd questions about the year she was born, leaving the house at hours Maren could not later account for. Maren had not asked. The etiquette is not to ask. She had told herself she would ask after the Review and it would be a calmer conversation. There was no after.

## Lie they believe

*If I had broken the etiquette and asked her, she would not have died.* The lie is that her silence killed her mother. The lie protects Maren from the larger and unbearable truth: that the Wing killed her mother on purpose, and that no amount of asking would have stopped it. The lie keeps the harm small and locatable in herself. The climax forces her to put it down — and at that price to look directly at an enemy she cannot defeat in one book.

## Ghost

The Saturday morning before her mother's last Review. Eira at the kitchen window, scalp port catching the cold light, telling Maren — without looking at her — that there was a particular row of plum trees in Lowmaren that Maren should walk to one day, the third tree from the south end, and that Maren would understand when she got there. Maren had said *okay* and gone to school. She has never walked to the trees. She does not know what is at the third tree. She has not been able to make herself find out.

## Competence

- **Pattern-reading bodies.** Maren can tell a person's state from twenty paces — gait, hand position, the angle of a shoulder. She does not know she is doing it. Her aunt Lo, who is a port-maintenance technician, taught her to read bodies the way clinicians read them, casually, across the kitchen table. This is the skill the plot will tax: she can read Sael as a tool that knows it is being aimed.
- **Stillness.** Maren can stand without moving for long stretches; she learned it in the year after her mother died when crying made the apartment worse and stillness made it bearable. In the plot this becomes a usable skill (hiding, surveillance, the alley) — it reads to Sael's Truth Lattice differently than ordinary stillness, and he notices.
- **Small-blade work in the kitchen.** A paring knife in her hand is a familiar object. She has done a thousand evenings of vegetables with one. The knife in the alley is not an accident of weapon. This is part of the frame against her.

## Contradiction

She photographs obsessively, like a compliant Rememberer, and she has never told anyone — including her aunt — that she does it. The wall above her cot is blank. The photographs are inside the wall, taped to the back of the loose pine board behind the lamp. She does not look at them. She just needs to know they are there.

## Flaw

She mistakes her own moral self-suspicion for moral seriousness. When a thing goes wrong she immediately looks for what she did to cause it. This is admirable in small contexts and catastrophic in this case — the frame works because she is already willing to convict herself before she has the facts. The plot exploits this in every Sael-interview scene.

## Voice

- **Vocabulary:** Cant working-class, Tier Three. Plain Saxonate words, occasional pharmacy-Latin picked up from Lo (resin, sealant, port, infusion). Avoids the State's polite register — does not say *kindness*, says *the take* in private and nothing at all in public. No profanity beyond *damn* and *for the city's sake*, the latter said with bitterness Sael will eventually clock.
- **Sentence length default:** Bimodal toward short. Many fragments. Occasional long sweeping sentence when she is touching something tactile she trusts (her aunt's hand, the railing of the Helvet Stair, the kitchen knife). Long sentence = sensory comfort. Short sentence = thinking under pressure.
- **Avoids talking about:** her mother by name. Her own competence (she will not say *I noticed*; she will say *anyone would have seen*). What is taped behind the pine board. Sael's age, after the first interview.
- **Speech tics:** Restates a question as a fragment before answering. ("What I did. I don't — I had the knife. I had the knife in my hand.") Says *I think* in front of statements that turn out to be true and omits it in front of statements that turn out to be false; a tell Sael's Lattice catches by chapter five.
- **Subtext default:** When stressed, she goes quieter and more sensory. She does not joke under pressure. She does not perform calm; she becomes physically still, which reads as calm to almost everyone except Sael and Lo.

## Physical

Seventeen. Short for her age, narrow through the shoulders, with the wiry forearms of someone who has carried a Lowmaren grandmother's grocery loads up four flights of Helvet Stair her whole life. Black hair she cuts herself at the nape with kitchen scissors — uneven, deliberately. Pale port-resin behind her left ear like every Tier Three; the resin is slightly yellowed on her, an old discoloration she cannot account for and has never asked about (Lo could tell her, doesn't). A small chemical burn on the back of her left wrist from helping Lo at the clinic at fourteen. After each Annual Consolidation Review she wears a recovery sleeve on the left wrist over the IV-access site for several days — the small clinical detail of the Wing's procedure that even Tier Three citizens carry home (ratified per ch 1 continuity pass). She walks heel-first, soft, like someone who has learned not to be heard in a hallway.

## Arc beats

- **Planted:** ch 1-3 — Maren wakes with three remembered days and immediately interrogates herself before interrogating the world. The first interior beat in chapter 1 is her cataloguing what she has done wrong, not what has been done to her. Lo asks her if she's eaten; she lies. The lie is small and the lie is the arc.
- **Tested:** ch 11-14 (midpoint inversion zone) — The case turns. Evidence accumulates that the body was placed for her to find and Sael was placed to find her. Her instinct is still to ask *what did I do to be chosen*. The Rememberer, Pell, finally tells her that her mother's death was not cardiac. She refuses to believe it at first because believing it costs her the lie she has lived inside since twelve.
- **Resolved:** ch 22-24 (climax) — Alone with Sael on the question of what to do with what they both now know, Maren says her mother's name out loud for the first time in the book. The sentence has no hedge and no fragment. She names the killer correctly — the Wing — and accepts that this means she cannot fix it in one book and cannot fix it with her own hand. She chooses to live remembering, not to die proving. The cost: Sael does not follow her over that line, and the alliance she could have had ends in a stalemate.

## Knowledge

Initial state at book open (continuity-checker owns updates after drafting begins):

- **knows:**
  - She had her third annual Review eleven days before chapter 1 opens.
  - Her aunt Lo works at Clinic Andren-Vell in Lowmaren.
  - Her mother Eira died five years ago, officially of cardiac failure during her fifteenth Review.
  - She, Maren, took stitch in the week before her third Review. She does not know who gave it to her or how it got into her possession. She remembers taking it. She does not remember acquiring it.
  - The general etiquette of not-asking, the existence of carry-notes (which Tier Three cannot get), the children's rhyme, the basics of the Tier system as Cant grandmothers describe it.
  - The Wing is the institution that does the Reviews. The Wing has Inquisitors. The Inquisitors are dangerous.
- **believes (incorrectly):**
  - That her mother died of natural cardiac failure.
  - That her silence — her failure to ask Eira what was happening in the week before — contributed to her mother's death.
  - That the three remembered days were a side-effect of stitch she chose to take and that the responsibility is therefore hers.
  - That the man in the alley is someone she might have known and forgotten through a previous Review. (He is not. She has never met him.)
  - That if she can identify what she did in those three days, she can decide whether to turn herself in or run.
  - That the Wing is bureaucratic, callous, and uniform — not that it has interior factions and a hidden directorate.
- **doesn't know:**
  - That her mother was a refusal-response carrier and was monitored, not lost, for fourteen years.
  - That her mother's death was a deliberate cascade.
  - That the body in the alley was a Wing W-I technician, placed for her to be found with.
  - That Sael was assigned to her by someone inside the Wing who wants Sael himself destabilized.
  - That stitch is produced inside the same manufactories that produce Lethonal-7.
  - That Pell, the Rememberer she will meet, sold her the stitch she took before her last Review on Lo's quiet referral, and that this was the third time Pell has done so.
  - The existence of Wing-Internal, the Cold Archive, Floor Minus Eleven, or Andren Sault.

### Updates after ch 1 (continuity-checker, post-draft)

- **now holds, but has not reported:** there is a dead adult man at the dark end of Sennet's Alley with a single wound below the third button of his grey wool coat, frost on his sleeve (cold has had time — not minutes); she does not know him; she has set the paring knife from her hand wrapped against her own forearm inside her cuff and carried it home up Helvet Stair.
- **now actively present, not theoretical:** the three remembered days are surfacing at angles since the second morning after Review. One drift-fragment has now surfaced on-page (S-023 seed): bread-smell on the Lowmaren wind, a doorway with a metal lip, a hand near her shoulder not touching her, the lip of the doorway high enough she had to step up. She does not yet know whose hand or which doorway.
- **rhyme registered as menace for the first time:** the third line — *one little day for the city's sake* — has gone into Maren in a place it had never gone before. She has not articulated what she now hears in it.
- **first on-page lie to Lo:** "I had bread on the way. At the corner." (Lo poured tea; Maren has not removed her coat; the knife is still wrapped against her forearm inside her cuff.)
- **the empty third slot in the kitchen knife block** has been seen and not-named: Maren registered the size of the slot matches the size of the knife wrapped against her forearm; she has chosen not to look at the block twice and not to think about which knife has the chip at the heel.
- **knowledge route-of-Lo ratified:** Lo told her once at the table that the dead end of Sennet's Alley has no camera. Maren had filed it without filing it; on the night of ch 1 she registered that *someone else had also filed it and used it.*
