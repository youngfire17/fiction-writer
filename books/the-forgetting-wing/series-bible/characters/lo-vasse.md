# Lo Vasse

role: guardian / ally with withholdings / mirror foil to Maren
pov: no
arc: positive (slow, mostly off-page; surfaces in ch 19-20)
first-appearance: chapter 1
aliases: Aunt Lo (Maren); Citizen Vasse (Wing register); Lo (Pell, Iren on the rare occasion their paths cross professionally)

## Want (external goal)

To keep Maren safe through one more Review cycle without Maren learning what Lo has spent five years not telling her — specifically, that Eira's death was not cardiac, that Lo knew it was coming, and that Lo failed to stop it. Lo's day-to-day Want is small and tactical: keep the flat warm, keep the photographs on the wall current, keep the stitch supply running through Pell so that Maren has some chance of remembering anything Lo has been unable to say aloud.

## Need (internal truth)

To accept that she cannot protect Maren by managing what Maren knows, and that the protection has been, in fact, a continuation of the silence that killed Eira. She needs to tell Maren the things Eira asked her to tell her — five years late.

## Wound

The week before Eira's fifteenth Review. Eira came to Lo's clinic — Clinic Andren-Vell, Lowmaren — through the back door, at the close of shift. She told Lo, in the dispensary, that this Review would not be a normal one. She had been visited at home, twice, by someone who said his name was Telleg (no relation to Iven; a fabricated cover the W-I scheduling office used for direct-citizen contact in those years). Eira asked Lo to take Maren if she did not come back. Lo asked the obvious questions. Eira gave her three sentences and asked her not to ask more. Lo did not ask more. She has spent five years building a case in her head for why she did not ask, and the case has gotten thinner every year.

## Lie they believe

*Withholding is a kind of love when the alternative is danger.* Lo has built her whole adulthood on a Tier-Three Cant version of this lie — you do not ask, you do not tell, you preserve the next generation by routing them around what would harm them to know. The lie is plausible because it has, sometimes, worked. It did not work with Eira and it is not working with Maren, but Lo has not yet been able to face that.

## Ghost

Lo at twenty-three, the night her own first Review came back wrong — a smearing event that took four days instead of one, that the clinic she worked at then logged and did not report. She has the smearing in her body — she misses, mildly, the names of two cousins she used to know — and she made a decision, alone in her flat that month, that she would never trust the Wing again and would also never refuse it again. The shape of that decision is the shape of her life. She passed the shape to Eira, who refused it. Eira's refusal is why Eira is dead. Lo cannot say this even to herself in those words.

## Competence

- **Port maintenance and clinic-front concealment.** Lo can read a port for hairline cracks at three meters, dress a sealant failure in under ten minutes, and run the dispensary's front room with no record of who came in for what. She is the third-most-senior tech at Clinic Andren-Vell and the only one with consistent access to both the front and the back room.
- **Reading bodies.** She taught Maren this skill — casually, across the kitchen table, twenty years of meals — without naming what she was teaching. The plot taxes this when Lo, in ch 7, reads Sael at the door and knows what he is before he names himself.
- **Cant-side trust networks.** Lo knows seventeen households on Helvet Stair by first name and which one of them will hide a thing that needs hiding.

## Contradiction

She is a devout participant in the Civic Memorial at the Sequence every year. She walks down with a white flower and stands among the Tier Three families and weeps openly — not for the names she does not know on the tiles but for the names she does not see on the tiles and should. The other Cant mothers think she is sentimental. Maren has never asked why Lo cries; she has been embarrassed by it her whole life. The reader will eventually understand. (The drafter may stage this scene once.)

## Flaw

She has organized her relationship with Maren around the principle of *not now*. Every difficult conversation is deferred to a later date that has never arrived. The flaw is that *not now* has become *not ever*, and the unsaid things have accumulated to the point where saying them feels worse than continuing to not say them. The plot exploits this in ch 19, when Lo will be asked, plainly, by Maren, to say what she knows, and Lo will hesitate one beat too long.

## Voice

- **Vocabulary:** Cant working-class, Tier Three, with technical pharmacy-Latin (resin, sealant, port, infusion, mirror-recall — a word she should not know and uses once a year). Plain Saxonate underneath. Some Lowmaren-side slang from her childhood (*the cut, the lift, the bread-wind*).
- **Sentence length default:** Medium and complete. She finishes her sentences. The completion is part of her competence-as-mask. When she is genuinely shaken, she goes shorter — a Lo who fragments her sentences is a Lo who is about to tell the truth.
- **Avoids talking about:** Eira by name (matches Maren's avoidance, which is partly inherited from Lo). Her own first Review and the smearing. The visits from Telleg. The pine board in Maren's room (she knows about the photographs; she has never said).
- **Speech tics:** *Eat something* — to Maren, eight or ten times a day, as both literal nutrition and verbal placeholder for *I love you and I cannot say it*. *That's a question for after the Review* — used to defer almost anything; the line becomes corrosive over five years.
- **Subtext default:** When stressed, she becomes more domestic. She will fold a towel in the middle of a difficult conversation. She uses physical labor to slow the moment.

## Physical

Forty-six. Stocky, broad-shouldered, the kind of Cant frame that handles four flights of Helvet Stair with groceries and does not slow. Greying brown hair pulled back into a clip Maren bought her at twelve. Port behind her left ear is older, dulled by years of clinic resin getting on her own hands. A long thin scar on the inner left forearm from a sealant burn at twenty-eight. She keeps her sleeves rolled to mid-forearm at home and to the wrist when she leaves the flat. She walks heavily, deliberately, the opposite of Maren — the sound of Lo coming up the stair is one of the safe sounds of the book.

## Arc beats

- **Planted:** ch 1, 2, 5 — Lo's *eat something*, her closed face when Sael comes to the door, her refusal to ask Maren about the eleven days. The deferral is the texture of every scene with her in the first act.
- **Tested:** ch 14-15 — Maren brings the case home — figuratively; she is being followed and will not say. Lo asks the wrong question (*Did you take stitch*) and Maren answers honestly (*Yes*) and Lo cannot ask the next one (*Who gave it to you*). The deferral fails. Maren leaves the flat the next morning without explanation.
- **Resolved (partial; arc completes off-page after book end):** ch 19-20 — Lo finds Maren at Pell's. She tells Maren, finally, three of the things Eira asked her to say five years ago. Not all of them. The breakthrough is real and incomplete. Maren will hold this conversation against Lo for some chapters and will, by ch 24, understand it. Lo will, by the end of the book, have begun to attend a Rememberer cell — a thing she would not have done at chapter one.

## Knowledge

- **knows:**
  - Eira was a refusal-response carrier. (She does not have the Wing's term; she calls it *what happened to her*. She knows the substance.)
  - Eira's death was not cardiac. (She does not know it was a deliberate cascade; she believes it was a botched Review the Wing did not investigate.)
  - Maren has taken stitch. (She knows the source — Pell — and the cadence. She has facilitated the supply at one remove.)
  - The Telleg-cover name was used by the Wing to make pre-Review house visits in some years. (She does not know it is unrelated to Iven Telleg.)
  - Pell knew Eira. The depth of that connection, Lo only partly knows. (Pell and Eira were lovers for two years before Maren was born. Lo knows they were close. Lo does not know how close.)
  - The Wing's Cant-side surveillance has thinned in the last six months — fewer cohort cameras maintained, fewer port-scan checkpoints staffed. (She reads this as budget. It is not budget. It is something deliberate; someone above Iren is letting Cant breathe.)
- **believes (incorrectly):**
  - That she has been protecting Maren by withholding what she knows.
  - That Eira's death was a procedural failure, not an order.
  - That Pell is trustworthy in the matter of Maren. (Pell is trustworthy in most things; Pell has been lying about one specific thing — see Pell's sheet.)
  - That the Wing has no individual director.
- **doesn't know:**
  - Wing-Internal as a category.
  - The Cold Archive.
  - That Andren Sault is alive.
  - That her own first-Review smearing was not a clinic error but a Bureau-era residue — she was, technically, a fourth-cohort trial subject. The Wing has her flagged.
  - That Iven Telleg's surname being identical to the Wing's cover-name on Eira is a coincidence the orchestrator chose to exploit — Iven was, in part, picked as the body because his surname would resonate against Eira's old visitors and unsettle Lo if she ever heard it. (She will hear it in ch 12 and freeze, and Maren will see her freeze and not ask, and the deferral chain will tighten.)
