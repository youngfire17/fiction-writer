# Geography

Use for: any scene that establishes place, movement between districts, the Wing's building, or a named location the plot routes through. Load when blocking a scene or staging a chase. All addresses, district nicknames, checkpoints, and street fixtures listed here are canon — do not invent new ones; ask the world-builder to extend this file.

## The city

Halvern-Estre is a single-jurisdiction city-state on a cold inland lake at the foot of a low coastal range. Population ~1.4 million. Walled on the south and west by the range; walled on the east and north by the State, which maintains a four-kilometer cleared belt called the **Outer Cordon** beyond which civilian travel is unlicensed. Climate is long grey winters, short bright summers, persistent lake fog. The lake is called the **Plate**. The city is shaped like a half-moon pressed against the Plate's southern shore.

Districts are governed by the **Ministry of Civic Order**, but the Wing's chiller infrastructure cuts under every one of them — a citywide cold loop running at a frequency residents of the inner ring stop hearing within a few years. Visitors call it the Halvern hum.

## Districts

### Estre-Hand
type: district
region: inner ring / north shore
population: ~180k
The administrative core. The Wing's main building, the Ministry of Public Cognition's secondary offices, the Civic Archive, and the High Court all stand within a six-block grid. Wide pale-stone boulevards, no trees over six meters (none allowed — sightlines). Inquisitors live in subsidized housing on the eastern edge, in a block called **Tessen Row**. Streets are named after virtues in the State's older liturgy: Patience, Sequence, Continuance. Sael's apartment is on Continuance, third floor, north-facing.

### Lowmaren
type: district
region: south industrial / lake shore
population: ~310k
Working district. Cargo handling on the Plate, the carrothenidine and propofol manufactories, two textile mills, the State pharmacy depot. Built in the pre-Wing decade and never refurbished. Brick. Yellow stoneware roofs. Steam from the manufactories smells of warm bread for reasons no one has explained on-page. Maren's mother used to live here before the move to Cant. Lowmaren is where stitch is cut and filmed before it's smuggled into the upper districts.

### Cant
type: district
region: middle ring / west slope
population: ~240k
Mixed residential, the canonical lower-middle district. Narrow streets, vertical houses, communal courtyards in the rear, laundry strung between balconies that the Civic Order keeps trying to fine into compliance. Cant has the most photographers per capita in the city — the wall-density rule is most extreme here. Maren lives at **17 Helvet Stair**, fourth floor, in a flat she shares with her aunt Lo. The stairwell smells of boiled cabbage and the resin sealant the Wing uses on scalp ports — Lo works in a port-maintenance clinic.

### Vell-Outer
type: district
region: outer ring / north slope
population: ~390k
Sprawling residential overflow. Built after the Wing was founded, when the city's population grew faster than its center could hold. Identical six-story blocks in pre-cast concrete. Wide boulevards lined with cherry-laurel hedges. The two long-term care facilities for Bleeders (**House Andren** and **House Severeen**) are out here, behind hedges that are kept too high to see over. Most citizens have never visited Vell-Outer. The trams that run out here stop running at curfew.

### Marrow Quarter
type: district
region: inner ring / under-city
population: ~70k registered, more unregistered
The pre-Wing old town. Below grade in places — built into the foundations of the bishops' city the State demolished a century ago. Crooked streets, none of them on the official map. The only district where the chiller hum is inaudible because the original stone deflects the lattice. Counterculture lives here: the **Rememberers** (small cells of people who try to recover what was taken), the unported by choice, certain artists, the underground clinics. Surveillance is heavier here in personnel but lighter in instruments, because the Wing's biometric net does not penetrate the stonework. The Wing has been arguing for thirty years for permission to demolish the Marrow. The argument keeps not happening.

### Hael-by-Plate
type: district
region: north shore / elite
population: ~45k
Wealth. Pre-Wing villas, lake views, private piers. The senior Ministers, the Wing's department heads, the half-dozen industrial families. The Director of the Wing is believed to live here but no one in the book has met him. Hael citizens receive the **Tier-One** version of the Review (see cultures.md) and may, under sealed petition, learn the date of their taken day — never the content.

## The Wing

### The Forgetting Wing
type: building / institution
region: Estre-Hand
The institution is the **Anterograde Welfare Division** of the Ministry of Public Cognition. The building is colloquially the Forgetting Wing because of the literal architectural wing — a long curving annex pushing out from the older Ministry block, glass and pale stone, three stories above ground, eleven stories below. The public sees the upper three: the intake foyer (warm laundry smell, soft music, pamphlets), the Review theaters (clean white, low light, the contoured headsets), the recovery bays. The lower eleven hold the technicians' quarters, the cartridge fabrication labs, the Cold Archive (where the threaded engram data is allegedly destroyed but in practice retained), the Inquisitors' floor, and at the bottom level something the bible records here but no citizen has confirmed: **Floor Minus Eleven**, accessible only by a single dedicated lift that requires two-key clearance. (See iceberg.md.)

The Wing's exterior is famously beautiful — citizens take wedding photographs in front of it. The compressors that vent the chiller heat are routed into the Plate, raising the lake's surface temperature by a measurable fraction. The fish on the Wing-side shore taste different. Lowmaren families won't eat them.

## Specific locations the book uses

### Sennet's Alley
type: alley
region: Cant, off Helvet Stair, two blocks down
The alley where Maren wakes with the body. Narrow, dead-ending at a brick wall behind a closed dye-works. Smell: wet brick, old indigo, the bread-steam from the Lowmaren wind. A single working sodium lamp at the mouth, dark at the closed end. The body lies at the dark end. No camera covers the dead end — a Civic Order maintenance gap that has been logged and unfixed for three years. Someone knew that.

### Clinic Andren-Vell
type: clinic
region: Lowmaren
A small port-maintenance clinic where Lo works and where stitch can be obtained by people Lo trusts. The front room treats real complaints (irritation, sealant failure, infections); the back room is the dispensary. Lo is not the dealer. Lo knows who is.

### The Sequence
type: public square
region: Estre-Hand, fronting the Wing
The plaza where the annual Civic Memorial is held. Black granite, set with 14,000 small brass tiles, one for every citizen the State officially records as having "given a day for the city" in the previous year — the Bleeders, the Cascades, the rare deaths in theater. The tiles are unnamed. Citizens leave white flowers. The square is also where Sael was sworn in.

## Movement between districts

- **Standard travel:** any citizen with valid papers may travel between districts during daylight (06:00-22:00). Trams run on five lines. Biometric checks at the inner ring borders (Estre-Hand and Hael-by-Plate) — a port-read at the gate, two-second verification.
- **Curfew:** 22:00-05:00, no movement between districts without a stamped night-pass. Within a district, walking is technically allowed but invites questioning.
- **Marrow Quarter:** no biometric gates, because no infrastructure. Entry is by foot through one of six recognized ingresses. The Wing tolerates this; it also surveils the ingresses with human watchers.
- **The Cordon:** civilians do not cross. Freight crosses under bonded escort. What is outside the Cordon is not the book's problem yet (see iceberg.md).
